My Process

Research

Research and studying competitors is a cruial step to take to understand what's expected of your prodcut and how to improve on what's already been made.

I search Google as though I'm a client looking for the type of prodcut I'm creating and see what's out there. Once I;ve made my assessments I start sketching.



Sketch

I prefer to create layout in a physical notebook and always a mobile first approach.

At this point I'm thinking of how the user will navigate my site or app. I pay close attention how the layout helps usesrs find what they need quickly, how what what I'm displaying is cause the user tot think and and interacte with the content.

I'm thinking of what feelings I want to provoke in the user. Am I seelling something? find wasy to include additional informatio about the product or service. Am I sharing thoughts? Use more casual language and make the focus the words being read.

This is also the place I play around with different ideas. Nothing is concrete until it is.


Wireframes

Once the layout is done I open Figma and begin making wireframes. I can go further with prototypes but since I'm primarily a solo dev I skip that step.

Here I decide color schemes and images to use. There's ideas that I scribble down in the sketch phase but here is where they get to be realized.


Scripting

Finally, comes the part that brings the project to life. I create my Git repository and clone into my local machine. Once done I get to work.

I start with the index and create pages based in the nav if ones included. I prefer to work with React when creating apps or larger sites, otherwise I stick to basic HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

Sticking to the basics comes in handy when working with Wordpress sites as I'm usually adjusting css styles that I need to tweak.


After weeks, more often months, of work I have a completed product.

If you'd like to see my works take a look through my Github or the case studies displayed here.
