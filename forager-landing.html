Misidentifying a plant isn't a small mistake — and forgetting where you found the good patch is its own kind of loss. Forager fixes both.

Three steps, every time you go out

Forager's Log follows the same path every forager already walks — spot something, check it, remember it.

01
Identify

Snap it. Know it.

Photograph any plant or fungus and get an instant ID, complete with a plain-language description and an at-a-glance edibility rating — so you're never tasting first and asking questions later.

02
Log the spot

Never lose a good patch.

Drop a pin with the exact coordinates, note the plant growth around it, and attach a photo. Every location you add gets reviewed and verified before it's shared.

03
Build your log

Your foraging history, mapped.

Every find and every location lives in your personal log — searchable by plant, by use, or by the trail you found it on. Your own field guide, built as you walk.

A rating built on danger and usefulness

Danger and usefulness aren't opposites — some of the most useful plants are also the most dangerous. Forager's badge weighs both, so the number tells you something a color alone never could.

1
High danger, low useLittle to no upside — steer clear entirely.
2
High danger, high useValuable but unforgiving — read every note before acting.
3
Low danger, low useGenerally harmless, not especially worth seeking out.
4
Low danger, high useSafe and rewarding — the plants worth learning first.
